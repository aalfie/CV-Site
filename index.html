I am Alfie Strickland

Software engineer, BSc (Hons).

About me

 An interest in tech has been the main driving force in becoming a software engineer. From a young age I was always looking to build my own PC to fulfil my gaming hobby as well as regular day to day use. I managed to scrape the funds together from a part time job at 15 to finally build my PC after months of researching the best parts for my price range, but on completion, realised that this was the beginning of a whole new fascination of wanting the newest and best components possible. 

Due to the lack of money to spend on my own PC, I found myself instead convincing friends and family to invest in a new PC that I could build for them which would be better and cheaper than PC’s on the market.

Eventually, I decided to persue a career in software engineering as it would potentially allow me to work with the latest tech in the future. I joined university with very little programming experience, but quickly got to grips with the basics in Python, creating a (extremely buggy) connect four game in console.

Over the next few years, I had to develop a range of programs in different languages on my own, and sometimes as part of a small team. I was becoming increasingly comfortable with programming due to learning from others and due to writing more complex code such as making a binary search tree from scratch in C++, I was beginning to understand how programs stored data and how data is accessed through pointers.

The Future

Now, I'm in a service desk role. It has been fun learning learning about different issues an organisation or user can face when it comes to applications and systems not working as intended. It has been interesting to see that the debugging skills aquired from programming can be applied to troubleshooting user issues, which usually involves process of elimination and different testing methods. 

I have also had some chance to utilise my programming skills, where I have created various scripts to help automate some monotnous day to day tasks. In this role I have also been Repairing various devices including tablets, phones, monitors, laptops. I have learnt a lot from this as a lot of devices are built the same so the skill is very transferrable, and also helps to save costs, as the cost of repairs are much less than replacing each time a device has a minor fault.  

This role has also sparked an interest in infrastructure, I would be intrested in working with servers / networking within an organisation and maintaining larger business systems.
